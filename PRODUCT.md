# PRODUCT.md — NIL

## Register

brand

## Product purpose

NIL is a fictional wellness brand whose product line is the absence of products. It is a critical-design artifact from the MSc thesis *Già abbastanza / Already Enough — Self-Improvement Applications and the Manufacture of Deficit* (Efe Eren Can Bakir, MSc Design, Politecnico di Milano). The site is not a mockup of the brand; it is a real, inhabitable instance of it. Visiting the site should feel like encountering a high-end wellness brand and then realising, slowly, that it sells nothing and asks nothing of you.

## Users

- The thesis defence panel, who will open it live.
- Design-literate visitors who recognise the wellness-brand genre and feel the refusal land.
- Anyone who arrives expecting a product and leaves having read a position.

The site is read in a calm moment on a laptop or phone, not under task pressure. There is nothing to convert, sign up for, or complete.

## Brand and tone

Quiet luxury. Apothecary-calm. Sincere, never ironic, never parody. The register of Aesop, Frama, Kinfolk, Cereal: warm-neutral, editorial, unhurried, confident enough to leave space empty. The brand has nothing to prove and nothing to sell, and the design carries that posture.

## Voice

Short declaratives. The body read from inside, not as a score. No aspirational future tense. No call to action, no urgency, no "join", "start", "unlock". The two-line manifesto is the spine: *There is no version of you to optimise. You were already enough before we arrived.*

## Anti-references

- Loud DTC wellness brands: bright gradients, urgent CTAs, countdowns, streak and score UI, "your best self" copy.
- The tasteful-default web pairing (a Garamond revival + Inter loaded from the Google Fonts CDN). Competent, generic, forgettable.
- SaaS templates: hero-metric blocks, identical icon-heading-text card grids, modal-first flows.
- Any accent colour used to signal optimisation or alert.

## Strategic principles

- No accent colour, on principle. Contrast is carried by ink on warm neutral alone.
- No newsletter, no account, no app. The refusal extends into the site chrome.
- The site is the critique. Craft must be real, because a sloppy brand would read as parody and break the sincerity.
- Nothing to optimise: no progress, no metric, no gamification anywhere in the interface.
